# HR-Analytics-Dashboard-Excel-
📊 HR Analytics Dashboard - Employee Attrition Case Study
🎯 Project Overview
This project presents an end-to-end HR Analytics Dashboard built in Excel to analyze employee turnover (Attrition). The goal is to uncover the key drivers behind why employees leave the company and provide data-driven recommendations to the HR department to improve retention.

📈 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The dashboard monitors five primary metrics to provide an immediate health check of the workforce:
Total Employees: 1,470
Attrition Count: 237
Attrition Rate: 16.12%
Active Employees: 1,233
Average Age: 37 Years

💡 Key Insights (The "Story" Behind the Data)
Based on the analysis, here are the most critical findings:
1. 💸 Income & Financial Pressure
Critical Threshold: Employees earning between $1,000 - $4,000 monthly show the highest attrition rates (137 cases).
Correlation: As monthly income increases, the likelihood of leaving decreases significantly, suggesting that financial compensation is a primary driver for junior roles.

2. ⏳ The "New Manager" Risk
The 2-Year Gap: A massive 61.6% of resignations (146 out of 237) happen within the first 2 years of working under a specific manager.
Insight: This indicates a potential issue in the "Manager-Employee" relationship during the onboarding phase or a lack of early-stage leadership support.

3. ⚖️ Work-Life Balance (Overtime)
Overtime Impact: Employees working Overtime are much more likely to leave. Their attrition count (127) is higher than those who don't work extra hours (110), despite being a smaller group.
Observation: High-pressure environments are directly linked to talent loss.

4. 🏢 Department & Job Roles
High-Risk Roles: Laboratory Technicians (62), Sales Executives (57), and Research Scientists (47) are the top 3 roles with the highest attrition.
Department Focus: The R&D Department has the largest workforce but also the highest volume of staff movements.

5. 📍 Distance from Home
The Commute Factor: Employees living closer to the office (1-5 miles) have the highest attrition count (87), suggesting that proximity doesn't compensate for other negative factors like low pay.

🚀 Strategic Recommendations
To reduce the attrition rate, the following actions are recommended:
Targeted Retention Bonuses: Implement financial incentives specifically for employees in the lower income brackets ($1k - $4k).
Management Training: Provide specialized leadership training for managers focusing on the critical first 2 years of the employee-manager relationship.
Work-Life Balance Policy: Review the overtime policy to prevent burnout, particularly in high-attrition departments like R&D.
